# Intervals
Intervals are short periods of high intensity running, mixed with periods of lower intensity, recovery jogs. The idea is that by running faster than you can currently sustain, your body will experience the load and demands of that increased intensity, and adapt to better handle it in the future - which means you will be able to run faster! This targets the speed dimension of running fitness.

Intervals are probably the most diverse session, with many different plans or suggestions, but the aim of all of them is to run fast for some relatively short distance, allow your body to recover, and then run fast again.

Some famous sessions include: 
* Rob de Castellas "Deek's Quarters".  8 x 400m with 200m jog recovery between each. Deek used to run them in 61-62s - good luck with that!
* Steve Monaghetti's "Mona fartlek"
	* 2 x 90 seconds hard, 90 seconds float 
	* 4 x 60 seconds hard, 60 seconds float
	* 4 x 30 seconds hard, 30 seconds float
	* 4 x 15 seconds hard, 15 seconds float

But you can really make this whatever you like. The ideal pace to aim for is that your times are even, and the last one you do is the last one you _could_ do. Make sure you hold your form - this is training and not a life or death race. If you push so hard that your form suffers, you are trying too hard and need to slow down and / or allow more recovery time.

As you increase the load here, be careful to increase either intensity or distance, or decrease the recovery - but only one at a time. You either add cover more distance, or you push your body harder, not both.

Most runners have only a small part of their training as high intensity, so intervals should only form a small part of your overall weekly kilometres. This is because high intensity work is considered a high risk of injury - but they pack a punch! Think of is as the chilli powder of running, to be used sparingly. That metaphor also works because it hurts, sort of in a good way.
