It is ironic that running is not for the impatient. There are no shortcuts. The only way to improve at running is to do the work. Now there are some ways to do work that are more efficient and effective than others, and that is what I hope to share, but ultimately most people need to reset their expectations. Thinking you will see measurable improvements every week is unrealistic and will set you up for disappointment.
At most you might think of a monthly check in, but better is to think about where you want to be in 6 months or this time next year. 

Consistency is your friend. You want to be sustainable, consistent and enjoy the journey, not just focus on the destination. 

Trying to run too far, too fast, will guarantee illness and / or injury, forcing you to stop running and slowing down your long term progress. 

To give some perspective, a famous Australian running coach once said "after a runner has been on a high volume program (150kms+ per week) for three or four years, then you'll know what they are capable of".
